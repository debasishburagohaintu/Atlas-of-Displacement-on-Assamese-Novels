# Atlas-of-Displacement-on-Assamese-Novels

## Overview
A digital humanities project mapping refugee migration, literary geography, and Partition narratives through GIS and historical cartography.

## Objectives
- Visualize migrants and refugee movement
- Integrate literary geography with GIS
- Digitally reconstruct historical migration routes

## Research Context

**Atlas-of-Displacement-on-Assamese-Novels** is a digital humanities and literary GIS project that spatially maps migration narratives represented in Assamese fiction. Using geospatial technologies, historical cartography, and literary analysis, the project transforms textual journeys into interactive digital maps that visualize movement, displacement, routes, transportation networks, and place-based memory.

The project currently maps two Assamese novels representing two historically distinct forms of migration in Northeast India.

## *Upatyakar Para Upatyakaloi* — Deepak Kr. Barkakati

This novel portrays the forced migration of Bengali Hindu refugees in the aftermath of the Partition of India in 1947. The mapped journey traces overland displacement from Sylhet through Karimganj and Silchar to Nagaon, Assam. The digital map reconstructs refugee mobility across changing political borders and highlights experiences of displacement, uncertainty, and resettlement in post-Partition Northeast India.

## *Kanhibunar Malita* — Rudrani Sharma

In contrast, this novel depicts a voluntary migration that took place during the late nineteenth century. The mapped route follows movement from Mymensingh to Barpeta through the Brahmaputra river system. The project visualizes riverine mobility, water-based transportation, and migration networks that shaped social and cultural interactions in colonial Assam before the emergence of modern transport infrastructure.

## Digital Mapping Components

The interactive maps integrate:

- Georeferenced literary locations
- Migration routes and trajectories
- Historical and contemporary spatial layers
- Transportation methods (land and river routes)
- Place-specific quotations from the novels
- Migration chronology and spatial narratives

## Research Significance

The project contributes to the fields of Digital Humanities, Literary Cartography, Spatial Humanities, and Migration Studies by examining literature through geospatial interpretation. By combining textual analysis with GIS mapping, the project explores how literary narratives encode memory, mobility, borders, and historical geography across the Assam–Sylhet region.

Rather than treating migration solely as a literary theme, the project approaches migration as a spatial and historical process that can be visualized, interpreted, and comparatively studied through digital mapping technologies.
